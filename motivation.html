Andrew Huberman Podcast: Motivation

Summary

Image

Motivation is tightly woven with the neurochemistry of movement. The fundamental, recurring premise behind the science of motivation and movement is dopamine. Dopamine is responsible for wanting and craving; it is released in anticipation of something we want, and it dictates our desire to exert effort to achieve the thing we want. The main tool discussed to take advantage of the effects of dopamine to increase motivation is "dopamine scheduling".

Dopamine & Motivation

Image

We often analyse dopamine in the context of the reward pathway. The reward pathway begins at the Ventral Tegmental Area (VTA) which contains neurons that release dopamine. From this, the brain learns to associate a particular event with pleasure, resulting in the effect of the person wanting to do repeat that thing. However, the prefontal cortex is a part of the brain which acts as a break on the reward pathway, otherwise humans would simply be a pleasure-seeking species.
